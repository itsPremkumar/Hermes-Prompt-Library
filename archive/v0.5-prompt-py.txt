Hermes is a reasonable choice if its self-improvement and customization capabilities align with your goals, but I wouldn't say it is the only AI agent with self-improvement capabilities.

There are two different concepts here:

Self-improving workflow (practical today)

Self-improving AI model (not practical in the way people often imagine)

What Hermes can realistically do
If Hermes supports:

Updating its own documentation

Improving prompts

Refining workflows

Learning from project history

Saving new knowledge to GitHub

Reusing successful solutions

then it has a self-improving workflow. That's very useful and fits well with your AI Company OS.

However, that is different from the AI model changing its own neural network weights. Most current agents—including Hermes—do not retrain themselves autonomously during normal operation.

The architecture I recommend
Instead of making Hermes the irreplaceable center of the system, make it the default executive agent.

For example:

AI Company OS
    │
    ├── Executive Agent (Default: Hermes)
    ├── Coding Agent
    ├── Browser Agent
    ├── Research Agent
    ├── Memory Engine
    ├── Workflow Engine
    └── GitHub Knowledge Base
In this design:

Hermes coordinates the company.

GitHub stores the permanent knowledge.

Other agents can be added or replaced when they are better suited for a task.

Why this is better
Suppose that in two years:

Hermes is no longer maintained, or

another open-source executive agent becomes significantly better.

If your system is built around AI Company OS, you can replace the executive agent while keeping:

all documentation,

all workflows,

all checkpoints,

all prompts,

all business logic,

all accumulated knowledge.

Nothing is lost.

My recommendation
Use Hermes as the primary orchestration agent today if it best fits your needs, but avoid making the operating system depend exclusively on Hermes.

A good guiding principle for your prompt is:

"Hermes is the default executive AI agent responsible for coordinating the AI Company OS. The operating system itself remains model-agnostic and may integrate or replace agents as technology evolves, without losing accumulated knowledge or workflows."

That gives you the benefits of Hermes today while keeping your architecture adaptable to future advances in AI.
